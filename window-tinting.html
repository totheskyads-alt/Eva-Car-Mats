Reasons to tint

Four good reasons.

01

Appearance

Even, glass-deep tone with no orange peel or bubbling. The car looks finished.

02

Privacy

Cabin and contents stay out of view from the curb — particularly behind the B-pillar.

03

Heat

Ceramic films cut up to 88% of infrared. Cooler cabin, less air-con drain.

04

Glare

Low-light driving improves. Headlights and low winter sun stop blowing out the cabin.

Shade levels

Pick your darkness.

VLT — Visible Light Transmission — is the percentage of light the film lets through. Lower number, darker glass. We'll guide you to a level that's right for your vehicle, day-to-day driving and Irish road regulations.

Note · Irish law restricts how dark the front side windows and windscreen can be tinted. Rear windows are unrestricted on most vehicles. We'll advise on a fully road-legal configuration before any film is cut.

Process

Studio fit, four hours.

Tinting is one-shot work — there's no shortcut to a clean install. The studio is climate-controlled and dust-filtered for that reason.

01
Glass prep
Deep clean, edge inspection, mask trim. Any defect is corrected before film touches glass.
02
Computer-cut film
Pre-cut to your vehicle on a plotter — no blades near the paint. Each window cut individually.
03
Application & squeegee
Heat-shrunk, squeegee'd inside. Edges sealed flush to the glass with no light gap.
04
Cure & hand-back
2-hour cure in studio. Care sheet supplied. Leave windows up for 48 hours, drive away.
